About

I am a Computer Science undergraduate at Sichuan University (Pittsburgh Institute) with a profound passion for the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Artificial Intelligence (AI). My research focuses on turning complex, unstructured ideas into practical, human-centered systems. I am particularly interested in how we can leverage Knowledge Graphs and Large Language Model (LLM) reasoning to build recommendation and interaction systems that are not only accurate but also interpretable, safe, and aware of real-world constraints.

I believe that AI should empower people in their daily lives. By bridging multimodal signals, structured domain knowledge, and empathetic dialogue, I strive to design systems that feel natural to interact with and provide quantifiable, trustworthy assistance in critical domains like healthcare and education.

Selected Work

Health-Promoting Kitchen Assistant for Aging Population Jan 2026 – Present

A novel HCI-AI system designed to assist elderly individuals with chronic diseases in adopting healthier cooking practices through persuasive technology and personalized nutrition.

  • Recommendation Engine: Built the core engine integrating food knowledge graphs with medical dietary guidelines, enabling constraint-aware, health-oriented recipe suggestions.
  • Full-Stack Architecture: Engineered the backend APIs and frontend interface with a strong focus on accessibility, laying the groundwork for multimodal capabilities.
  • Multimodal Interaction: Designing vision pipelines for ingredient detection and voice pipelines using LLMs for natural, hands-free conversational guidance in the kitchen.
  • Empathetic Companionship: Currently incorporating an empathetic dialogue agent to improve user adherence, trust, and overall quality of life.
EduRiskX — Interpretable Student Risk Prediction Framework Oct 2025 – Feb 2026

A neuro-symbolic framework combining deep time-series modeling with rule-supported reasoning for early and interpretable academic risk detection.

  • Architecture Design: Designed and implemented EduRiskX, integrating Transformer-based time-series modeling with F-Logic reasoning for transparent explanations.
  • Performance Breakthrough: Achieved early detection on the OULAD dataset, identifying at-risk students up to 19 weeks in advance—substantially earlier than strong deep baselines like PatchTST and iTransformer (typically 15–16 weeks).
  • Actionable Insights: Maintained high AUC, F1-Score, precision, and recall while providing theory-aligned explanations and actionable intervention strategies for educators.
West China Hospital Biomedical Big Data Center · Research Assistant Intern Jun 2025 – Nov 2025

Collaborated with the Department of Mental Health to develop an intelligent auxiliary diagnosis system for mental health assessment.

  • Knowledge Graph Construction: Extracted triples based on PrimeKG to build a domain-specific knowledge graph, aiming to create a comprehensive medical literature QA system.

Intelligent Recipe Recommendation System May 2025 – Sep 2025

A Knowledge Graph and RAG-based system designed to handle fuzzy user queries with strict nutritional awareness and safety constraints.

  • Data Integration & Parsing: Integrated USDA nutrient data with the Recipe1M+ dataset. Utilized a 1.5B parameter LLM for complex intent parsing.
  • Safety & Constraint Verification: Implemented an agentic critic mechanism for safety verification, achieving a 98.5% constraint satisfaction rate.
  • Retrieval Performance: Achieved 0.825 semantic recall@5 on complex fuzzy intents, surpassing traditional dense retrieval methods by 50%.
Multimodal Mental Health Interaction System & LLM Reasoning Optimization Oct 2024 – Dec 2025
